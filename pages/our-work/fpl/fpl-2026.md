On June 10, 2026, the Gulf Coast Ecosystem Restoration Council (RESTORE Council or Council) voted to commit $403,654,000 in new funding for water quality improvement, habitat conservation and restoration, and other ecosystem projects and programs across the Gulf Coast, pursuant to the Resources and Ecosystems Sustainability, Tourist Opportunities, and Revived Economies Act of the Gulf Coast States of 2012 (33 U.S.C. § 1321(t) and note) (RESTORE Act). The [final 2026 Funded Priorities List (FPL)](https://www.restorethegulf.gov/~assets/2026_final__fpl_with_appendices_20260610.pdf) includes 19 ecosystem restoration projects and programs which build upon over $685 million in restoration activities previously identified by the Council. With these additional funds, the RESTORE Council has committed over $1 Billion for restoration activities across the Gulf Coast under the Council-Selected Restoration Component of the RESTORE Act. In addition, the RESTORE Council reallocated $9,605,860 in savings from previously approved projects and programs to further support activities in this FPL, which also includes updates to the RESTORE 2022 Comprehensive Plan. 

Before voting on whether to approve funding, the Council published the draft 2026 FPL amendment for a 43-day public comment period beginning on November 20, 2025 and ending January 2, 2026. During this time, the Council provided an overview of the proposed 2026 FPL via live public webinars and in person meetings. The public provided feedback during the public comment period, both verbally and in writing during public webinars. In general, all comments received, including attachments and other supporting materials, are part of the public record and subject to public disclosure. The Council reviewed the public comments received before the close of the public comment, then developed the [Response to Public Comments.](https://www.restorethegulf.gov/~assets/final-response-to-comments-20260610_aod.pdf) 

The Council appreciates those stakeholders who remain interested in Gulf restoration and participate in the Council's restoration activities by offering comments. If you are interested in receiving notifications of upcoming webinars, public meetings, or public comment periods, please [subscribe to receive the Council’s automatic email updates](https://public.govdelivery.com/accounts/USRESTORECOUNCIL/subscriber/new). There are options to select the “Public Meetings and Public Comment Periods” category, in addition to other categories of interest to you.
